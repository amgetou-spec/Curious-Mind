Eritrean Name

The name Eritrea origina tes from the ancient Greek term "Erythraia" (Ἐρυθραία), meaning "red" (erythros), referring to the Red Sea. This was then adopted into the Latin form "Mare Erythraeum". The Italians, during their 19th-century colonization, named the colony Eritrea, taking the name from the surrounding body of water.

Main Cities

Asmara

Asmara is the capital and largest city of Eritrea, located in the country's central highlands at an elevation of over 2,300 meters. It is known for its exceptionally well-preserved modernist architecture from the Italian colonial period, which led to its designation as a UNESCO World Heritage site. The city's urban landscape combines the features of its geographical context with a unique blend of modernist architecture, including art deco cinemas, Futurist buildings, and a well-defined city plan.

Massawa

Massawa or Mitsiwa (/məˈsɑːwə/ mə-SAH-wə)[a] is a port city in the Northern Red Sea region of Eritrea, located on the Red Sea at the northern end of the Gulf of Zula beside the Dahlak Archipelago.[2] It has been a historically important port for many centuries. Massawa has been ruled or occupied by a succession of polities during its history, including the Dahlak Sultanate, the Ottoman Empire, the Khedive of Egypt and the Kingdom of Italy. Massawa was the capital of the Italian Colony of Eritrea until the seat of the colonial government was moved to Asmara in 1897.[3] Massawa has an average temperature of nearly 30 °C (86.0 °F), which is one of the highest experienced in the world, and is "one of the hottest marine coastal areas in the world."[4]

Karen

Keren (Tigrinya and Tigre: ከረን, Arabic: كرن, Italian: Cheren), historically known as Sanhit,[2] is the second-largest city in Eritrea. It is situated around 91 kilometres (57 mi) northwest of Asmara at an elevation of 1,590 metres (5,220 ft) above sea-level. The city sprawls on a wide basin surrounded by granitic mountains on all sides. It serves as the capital of the Anseba Region, and is home to a number of ethnic groups including the Bilen people and Tigre people.[citation needed]

Eritrean Food

  1. Zigni/Sebi: A rich, spicy meat stew made with lamb, beef, or chicken and seasoned with berbere.
  2. Dorho: A spicy chicken stew with eggs and berbere, a staple at celebrations.
  3. Shiro: A vegan stew made from ground chickpea flour, onions, and tomatoes, known for its health benefits.
  4. Alicha: A mild, non-berbere dish featuring potatoes, carrots, and green beans with turmeric.
  5. Hamli: Sautéed collard greens with garlic, onions, and other herbs.
